# DECISION-MVE-STAKEHOLDERS-000 - Minimum Viable Experience (MVE) Stakeholder Expressions

- **ID:** DECISION-MVE-STAKEHOLDERS-000
- **Status:** Working Draft
- **Created:** 2026-03-23
- **Updated:** 2026-04-11

## Intent

Define stakeholder expressions specifically for the **Minimum Viable Experience (MVE)**, while inheriting the governance structure established in DR-GOV-000. This does not redefine stakeholders globally; it captures what we need to run and learn from the first experiences.

This record is guided by the Milieu Constitutional Core (Green Paper). Upon ratification, that reference will become CCR-000.

## Decision

Milieu experiences inherit the stakeholder and governance model defined in DR-GOV-000.

This record does not create a separate stakeholder system for Experience. Instead, it applies the existing governance structure to the Experience domain and identifies the experience-facing expressions that matter for design, facilitation, safety, authorship, and learning.

---

## 1. Inheritance from Governance

Within this Experience stakeholders are governed by the same model defined in DR-GOV-000:

- stakeholders are expressed as modes, not fixed classes
- contribution domains describe how value enters the system, not who a stakeholder is
- decision authority is scoped, not universal
- stewardship is responsibility, not ownership
- stakeholders act through human agents, AI agents, and system agents

This record should be read as a domain-specific interpretation of DR-GOV-000, not as a replacement for it.

---

## 2. MVE Stakeholder Expressions (Provisional)

Within the MVE, stakeholders may appear through provisional expressions such as:

**Audience / Guests**\
Entities observing, witnessing, responding to, or lightly participating in an experience. Some may later step into stronger forms of participation.

**Players / Actors**\
Entities participating directly in embodied or situated experience.

**World Builders / Storytellers / Producers**\
Entities shaping settings, narrative conditions, structures, and live or asynchronous experience elements.

**Facilitators / Guides**\
Entities helping maintain flow, invitation quality, coherence, safety, and recovery.

**Memory / Archivist functions**\
Entities or systems preserving continuity, recall, playback, and reusable context across sessions and time.

These are not separate stakeholder classes. They are **MVE-specific expressions** that may be occupied temporarily, jointly, or sequentially by the same stakeholder over time. Future experiences may use different expressions.

---

## 3. Movement Across Expressions

Experience design should assume movement across expressions.

Examples include:

- audience members becoming players
- players becoming world builders or facilitators
- creators participating in governance decisions affecting experience
- archivist or memory functions informing future experience design and continuity

This movement is desirable. Experience should support pathways between expressions without implying that any expression carries permanent authority.

---

## 4. Experience Contribution Domains

The contribution domains inherited from DR-GOV-000 remain applicable within Experience.

In this domain, they may appear as:

- **experiential** — presence, participation, feedback, meaning-making, witness, response
- **creative** — world building, storytelling, scene design, invitation design, performance, authorship
- **operational** — facilitation, moderation, safety, runtime support, continuity, archival support
- **financial** — funding, sponsorship, resource support, infrastructure support tied to experience delivery

These domains may overlap in practice.

---

## 5. Decision Scope in Experience

Experience decisions should remain scoped to the type of contribution being made.

Examples:

- experiential contribution informs quality, meaning, accessibility, and direction
- creative contribution informs story, world, interaction, and aesthetic structure
- operational contribution informs safety, continuity, reliability, and facilitation process
- financial contribution informs resource allocation related to experience delivery

No single contribution type grants universal authority over Experience.

Where Experience decisions cross into broader Milieu concerns, DR-GOV-000 remains the governing frame.

---

## 6. Stewardship in Experience

Stewardship within Experience is a responsibility to:

- protect participants
- maintain continuity
- enable contribution
- preserve psychological and relational safety
- support recovery when interactions fail or become harmful

Experience stewardship may be carried by guides, facilitators, moderators, producers, memory functions, or other contributors depending on context.

This does not create a separate ownership class inside Experience.

---

## 7. Agents in Experience

Experience stakeholders act through agents, including:

- human agents
- AI agents
- system agents

In Experience, agents may present as performers, facilitators, storytellers, memory systems, moderation systems, audience support systems, or other runtime functions.

These agents do not create new stakeholder classes. They operate under stakeholder authority and accountability.

---

## 8. External Stakeholders and Adjacent Participants

Experience may involve external stakeholders and adjacent participants such as:

- sponsors
- invited collaborators
- venue or platform operators
- safety partners
- supply-chain providers
- distribution partners
- outside audiences

As in DR-GOV-000, their standing is scoped to the value flows or constraints directly affecting the experience.

They do not gain general authority over Experience by default.

---

## 9. What This Record Adds

Relative to DR-GOV-000, this record adds only what is needed for the MVE:

- a small, testable set of stakeholder expressions for early experiences
- the expectation of movement across those expressions
- initial framing for contribution and stewardship in live, embodied contexts

These are provisional and expected to evolve or be replaced.

---

## Rationale (Condensed)

- Experience should inherit governance structure instead of inventing a parallel model
- familiar experience-facing expressions help designers and contributors orient quickly
- expressions are easier to test in live experience design than rigid stakeholder classes
- movement across expressions reflects the intended pathways within Milieu experiences
- stewardship and safety need explicit interpretation in embodied and participatory contexts
- memory and archivist functions matter in Experience even when not yet deeply specified

---

## Scope

Applies to:

- Minimum Viable Experience (MVE)
- early experience prototypes and facilitation models used to validate MVE

Does not define:

- a universal set of experience roles for all Milieu experiences
- the full governance model for Milieu
- detailed facilitation protocols
- safety mechanics or consent systems
- identity and trust systems
- detailed memory or archival architecture

---

## Open Questions

- Which experience-facing expressions prove most durable in practice?
- Should Audience and Guest remain combined or later separate?
- Should World Builder, Storyteller, and Producer remain grouped or later separate?
- How should memory and archivist functions be represented in live experience design?
- What pathways best support movement from audience to player to builder or facilitator?
- Which stewardship actions in Experience require explicit review or escalation?
