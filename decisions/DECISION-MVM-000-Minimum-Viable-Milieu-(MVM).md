# DECISION-MILIEU-MVM-000 — Minimum Viable Milieu (MVM)

**Status:** Working Draft\
**Created:** 2026-03-23\
**Updated:** 2026-03-23

---

## Intent

Define the minimum governance model required to operate Milieu, starting with the Minimum Viable Milieu (MVM), enabling multi-stakeholder participation while preserving continuity, fairness, and resistance to capture.

This document is guided by the Milieu Constitutional Core (Green Paper). Upon ratification, that reference will become CCR-000.

---

## Decision

Milieu governance operates as a multi-stakeholder stewardship model, expressed through modes of participation, contribution domains, and scoped decision authority.

---

## 1. Modes of Participation

Stakeholders are expressed as modes, not fixed roles:

**Participants**\
All entities engaging with Milieu.

**Contributors**\
Participants actively building, operating, or evolving Milieu.

**Stewards**\
Contributors responsible for safety, continuity, and governance.

Stakeholders may move between modes over time.\
No mode implies inherent ownership or authority.

---

## 2. Contribution Domains

Contributors may introduce value across domains, including:

- experiential
- creative
- operational
- financial

These domains describe how value enters the system, not who a stakeholder is.

---

## 3. Decision Scope

Decision-making authority is scoped to contribution domain.

- Financial contributions grant voice in resource and value allocation decisions
- Creative and operational contributions grant voice in system and experience decisions
- Experiential contributions inform quality, meaning, and direction

No contribution grants universal authority.

Stewards operate across domains to:

- maintain balance
- resolve conflicts
- protect continuity

---

## 4. Stewardship over Ownership

Governance authority is framed as responsibility, not control.

Stewards act to:

- protect participants
- maintain continuity
- enable contribution

No stakeholder group holds permanent ownership of Milieu.

---

## 5. Change via Proposals

All meaningful changes occur through Change Proposals.

Proposals:

- are visible by default
- allow input across stakeholders
- are resolved through defined processes (deferred)

---

## 6. Open by Default

Governance processes and decisions are open and observable by default.

Exceptions (safety, privacy, legal constraints) must be:

- explicit
- minimal
- auditable

---

## 7. Continuity and Anti-Capture

Governance must:

- resist domination by any single contribution domain or coordinated group
- prevent expansion of decision scope beyond defined domains
- preserve long-term continuity of Milieu

---

## 8. Right to Diverge

When alignment cannot be reached:

- stakeholders may diverge into separate paths or instances

Preference:

- support flourishing where possible
- divergence where necessary
- protection where required

---

## 9. Execution via Agents

Stakeholders act through agents, including:

- human agents
- AI agents
- system agents

Agents:

- do not constitute separate stakeholder classes
- operate under the authority and accountability of stakeholders
- must be identifiable and auditable where they perform governance actions

---

## 10. External Stakeholders and Value Flows

Stakeholders may exist both within and external to Milieu.

External stakeholders derive standing through:

- value exchange
- contractual relationships
- infrastructure or supply dependencies

External stakeholders:

- have scoped input where their value flows are directly affected
- do not gain general governance authority by default

Governance must:

- respect legitimate external constraints
- prevent external influence from expanding beyond its scope
- maintain continuity and autonomy of Milieu

---

## Rationale (Condensed)

- Modes avoid rigid class divisions
- Contribution domains allow multiple forms of value without hierarchy
- Scoped decision-making bounds influence while preserving voice
- Stewardship aligns authority with responsibility
- Agent model separates action from identity
- Open processes support trust and auditability
- Anti-capture preserves long-term viability

---

## Scope

Applies to:

- Milieu (starting with MVM)
- Early deployments such as MVE

Does not define:

- detailed decision mechanisms
- identity and trust systems
- full constitutional governance

---

## Open Questions

- How are proposals evaluated and resolved?
- How are contribution domains recognized or measured?
- What mechanisms enforce decision scope boundaries?
- How are safety interventions triggered and reviewed?
- How are agent actions authenticated and audited?
- How is capture detected and mitigated in practice?
