DR-MVE-000 - Minimum Viable Experience (MVE): Emergence First

Status: Working\
Created: 2026-03-26\
Updated: 2026-03-26\
Intent: Define the minimum conditions required to test whether an experience can emerge without prior instruction.

---

## Experience Overview

Enter a shared space with no instructions, no predefined identities, and no objectives. Speak, move, or gesture -- and watch the world respond. As signals overlap or align, clarity emerges. Together, participants discover what is possible, learn to coordinate without being told how, and unlock a transition that only shared action can achieve. Guides hold the space without directing it; observers witness meaning take shape. It feels intuitive, even magical -- because understanding follows interaction, not the other way around.

At its core, this is **play**: a shared, improvised space where participants explore, express, and co-create safely.

---

## Context

Milieu aims to support experiences where participants (including human, AI, and system participants) interact through shared environments without requiring predefined identities, knowledge of rules, or narratives.

Traditional approaches for both digital and tabletop roleplay (e.g., tutorials, session zero) front-load explanation and constrain behavior. This Minimum Viable Experience (MVE) tests whether participants can discover:

- what actions are possible
- how those actions affect the environment
- how coordination with others emerges

The first experience is an **initial encounter**, not a full session or predefined narrative.

---

## Value (MVE Scope)

This decision prioritizes value as **emergent experience** rather than predefined outcomes.

The MVE creates value by enabling participants to:

- discover what is possible without instruction
- express intent and see it affect a shared space
- coordinate with others to produce outcomes that were not pre-specified
- engage in **play** as a safe, shared act of exploration and improvisation

Value is realized through:

- agency (“I can act”)
- feedback (“the system responds”)
- emergence (“we created something together”)
- play (“we can explore without fear of getting it wrong”)

The MVE avoids:

- externally imposed goals
- reward structures as primary motivation
- explanations that replace discovery

This section makes explicit that the purpose of the MVE is not to deliver content, but to **enable meaningful interaction from which value arises**.

---

## Stakeholder Identities (MVE Scope)

These roles describe **modes of engagement**, not fixed identities.\
A participant may move between identities over time.

---

### Guide

Facilitates the experience.

May:

- hold space for interaction
- provide minimal prompts or framing
- support coherence and safety

Should not:

- define outcomes
- override participant agency
- prematurely resolve uncertainty

**Qualities of Experience (how value is felt):**

- shaping conditions for interaction without controlling outcomes
- observing emergence and responding with minimal intervention
- maintaining coherence while allowing surprise
- protecting a **safe space for play**

---

### Participant

Engages directly with the experience.

May:

- express intent through signals (voice, movement, gesture)
- explore actions through experimentation
- coordinate with others

Is not required to:

- define identity in advance
- understand system capabilities upfront

**Qualities of Experience (how value is felt):**

- discovering what actions are possible
- seeing signals produce visible effects
- influencing and being influenced by others
- engaging in **playful exploration without fear of failure**

---

### Observer

Witnesses the experience without direct participation (initially).

May:

- view the experience through a shared or directed perspective
- interpret interactions and outcomes
- learn from the system and participants

Should not:

- disrupt participant interaction
- dominate attention within the experience

May transition into Participant over time.

**Qualities of Experience (how value is felt):**

- interpreting unfolding interaction
- anticipating outcomes and shifts in coordination
- witnessing moments of clarity, alignment, or transformation
- experiencing the unfolding of **shared play**

## Decision

The Minimum Viable Experience will prioritize:

---

### 1) Emergent Interaction

Participants are not given explicit instruction on:

- roles
- capabilities
- objectives

Interaction is discovered through experimentation and feedback.

---

### 2) Signal-Based Interaction

All participant input is treated as **signals**, not commands.

Signals may include:

- voice
- movement
- gesture

Signals are interpreted with **varying confidence**, not binary success/failure.

---

### 3) Continuous Feedback

All participant actions should produce observable response.

Feedback should allow participants to infer:

- that an action occurred
- that the system responded

Feedback does not need to be explicit or explanatory.

---

### 4) Soft Coordination

The system encourages coordination without enforcing it.

Examples may include:

- overlapping signals reduce clarity
- aligned signals increase stability
- coherent interaction produces more reliable outcomes

Participants should be able to learn:

- turn-taking
- listening
- shared intent

without explicit instruction.

---

### 5) Minimal Participant States

Participants transition through a minimal set of states:

- not present
- present (partial or projected)
- embodied

State transitions are influenced by interaction, not granted upfront.

---

### 6) Guide as Facilitator

The Guide supports the experience without directing it.

The Guide may:

- introduce minimal prompts
- maintain pacing and safety

The Guide should allow participants to:

- initiate interaction
- discover outcomes
- shape progression

---

### 7) Observation Without Disruption

Observers may be present during the experience.

Observation should:

- not dominate participant attention
- not replace participant-to-participant interaction

---

### 8) Identity Emerges After Interaction

Participants are not required to define:

- character
- abilities
- backstory

Identity may emerge through:

- interaction
- behavior
- post-experience reflection

---

### 9) Reflectability (MVE Scope)

The experience should support reflection across Guides, Participants, and Observers.

This does not require recording or replay.

Reflection may be supported through:

- discussion
- shared recall
- lightweight summaries

Replay or recording may be introduced when required by:

- safety considerations
- learning or debugging needs
- stakeholder review or accountability
- inability to reconstruct events through reflection

---

### 10) Short, Complete Experience Loop

The MVE should:

- complete within a single session (\~45–75 minutes)
- include:
  - arrival
  - discovery
  - coordination
  - transition

---

## Experience Value (MVE Scope)

The experience should enable a sense of engagement (“fun”) through **discovery, expression, and shared emergence**, rather than predefined objectives or rewards.

The system should support:

- moments of surprise (“something happened that I did not expect”)
- increasing clarity (“I understand what I can do”)
- shared realization (“we did that together”)

The experience should avoid reliance on:

- explicit goals or win conditions
- external rewards or scoring systems

Engagement should arise from **interaction itself**, not from imposed structure.

---

## Experience Tone (MVE Scope)

The experience should maintain an **ambiguous ontological setting**.

Participants should not be able to determine:

- whether the experience represents past, future, or alternate realities
- whether it is technological, magical, or otherwise
- whether it exists within a single or multiple dimensions

To the extent that systems or mechanisms are perceived:

- they should feel intuitive and experiential, rather than explained
- advanced or unfamiliar processes may be experienced as magic-like

The experience should prioritize:

- sense-making through interaction
- interpretation through perception
- discovery over explanation

The underlying system should remain **setting-agnostic**, enabling future stakeholders to express a wide range of interpretations and worlds without requiring changes to core interaction models.

---

## Accessibility (MVE Scope)

Accessibility is a required consideration for all Milieu experiences, including the MVE.

The system should support a range of input and perception modes over time, enabling participation by agents with differing abilities, preferences, and constraints.

For the MVE, initial implementations may prioritize a limited set of interaction modalities (e.g., voice, movement, gesture) to validate core experience principles.

However:

- alternative input and output methods (e.g., text, assistive technologies) must be considered from the outset
- system design should not preclude future accessibility support
- accessibility is not optional and may be required to meet legal obligations in multiple jurisdictions

Where full accessibility support is not yet implemented, this should be treated as a known limitation rather than an intentional exclusion.

---

## Non-Goals (MVE)

Out of scope:

- predefined character systems
- complex UI or tutorials
- detailed narrative scripting
- fixed win/lose conditions
- full recording or replay systems

---

## Open Questions

- What level of signal ambiguity supports discovery without confusion?
- How much feedback is required for participants to infer capability?
- When does guidance become control?
- How do informed or dominant participants affect emergence?
- What role should observers play over time?

---

## Notes

Narrative framing (e.g., pods, projections, awakening states) may be used to support:

- incomplete identity
- gradual capability discovery

Such framing is optional and should not constrain future experiences.
