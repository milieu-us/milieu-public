## Intent

Capture Keith Johnstone’s work (*Impro: Improvisation and the Theatre*) as an upstream design reference for embodied play, invitation design, relational interaction, and safe role assumption.

This record does not prescribe implementation. It exists to inform future Experience design, evaluation, and experimentation.

This reference is held provisionally and may be revised, expanded, or removed based on direct experience and evidence.

---

## Summary

Keith Johnstone’s work on improvisational theatre explores how people enter states of responsive, embodied creativity when social fear and self-censorship are reduced. His exercises and frameworks focus on enabling spontaneous interaction, relational awareness, and emergent narrative through live collaboration.

For Milieu, this work is relevant because it describes mechanisms that allow participants to **enter roles deeply and interact in ways that feel authentic, relational, and meaningful**.

---

## Concepts Potentially Relevant to Milieu

### Status

Johnstone describes *status* as a dynamic signal expressed through posture, timing, voice, confidence, deference, and invitation.

Scenes become compelling when status relationships **shift, reverse, or evolve**.

**Possible Milieu implication**

Experiences may benefit from systems that:

* allow roles to shift status naturally
* support visible or subtle status signals
* reward recognition and response to status changes

---

### Offers and Acceptance

Improvisation advances when participants make **offers** — actions or statements that establish new information — and others accept and extend those offers rather than blocking them.

**Possible Milieu implication**

Design interaction systems that encourage:

* making offers
* accepting and building on others’ offers
* constructive recovery after rejected or misunderstood offers

---

### Reincorporation

Earlier elements of a scene are brought back later, giving the experience a sense of memory and coherence.

**Possible Milieu implication**

Systems may benefit from remembering earlier details and reintroducing them later to create meaningful continuity.

---

### Masks and Trance

Johnstone explored how performers sometimes enter deeper states of role assumption when external identity signals are reduced (for example through masks).

These states allow participants to **respond more directly through character and situation rather than conscious performance**.

**Possible Milieu implication**

Embodied play may become more meaningful when participants can:

* temporarily suspend everyday identity
* inhabit roles more deeply
* interact through character rather than meta-commentary

---

## Safety and Design Considerations

Embodied and trance-like states require careful design.

Important safeguards may include:

* explicit participant consent
* clear entry and exit mechanisms (de-roling)
* facilitator or director oversight
* psychological safety boundaries
* structured recovery if experiences become uncomfortable

These structures should be considered part of the **core architecture of play**, not optional features.

---

## Milieu Relevance

Johnstone’s work suggests that meaningful learning and insight can emerge through **embodied relational play**, not only through abstract narrative or scripted interaction.

Milieu experiences may benefit from supporting:

* embodied role assumption
* live co-creation between participants
* responsive invitation structures
* graceful recovery from failed interactions
