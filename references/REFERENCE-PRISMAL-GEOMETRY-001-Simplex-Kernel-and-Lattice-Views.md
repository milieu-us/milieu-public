# REFERENCE-PRISMAL-GEOMETRY-001 - Simplex Kernel and Lattice Views

- **ID:** REFERENCE-PRISMAL-GEOMETRY-001
- **Status:** Working Draft
- **Created:** 2026-04-27
- **Updated:** 2026-05-01

## Intent

Capture the current Prismal Space geometry research view that local simplex relations should be examined before selecting lattice realizations, coordinate systems, engine-facing data structures, or behavior profiles.

This reference exists to keep the tetrad/simplex starting point visible while still making room for Face Centered Cubic, Hexagonal Close Packing, Body Centered Cubic, cubic scaffolds, and Barlow stackings as views, realizations, or comparison structures.

## Context

REFERENCE-PRISMAL-003 currently defines the Prismal substrate kernel as a local close-packing rule rather than a named lattice. In 3D, the minimal non-degenerate local relation is four mutually touching sphere centers forming a regular tetrahedron.

That local relation can generate regular global realizations such as Face Centered Cubic and Hexagonal Close Packing, and broader Barlow-style stacking sequences. However, the local relation itself is not identical to any one global lattice.

This distinction matters for current Prismal work because earlier geometry discussions can easily collapse into selecting a coordinate convention, a lattice enum, or a rendering grid too early. Those may become useful views or implementation choices, but they should not be mistaken for the foundation.

## Provisional Claim

The current provisional starting point for 3D Prismal geometry is the Prismal Tetrad: four mutually touching equal sphere centers forming a regular tetrahedral simplex.

From this point of view:

- The Prismal Tetrad is a local relational primitive.
- A simplex complex is one way to preserve local adjacency and incidence.
- Barlow packings are global close-packed stacking sequences that can be generated from repeated local choices.
- Face Centered Cubic and Hexagonal Close Packing are regular global realizations within that close-packed family.
- Body Centered Cubic and simple cubic structures are important reference and implementation views, but they are not generated by the same close-packed stacking rule.

This is a research framing, not an implementation decision.

## Key Separations

### Local Relation Is Not Global Lattice

A Prismal Tetrad describes a local relation among four mutually adjacent sphere centers.

A lattice describes a global regular repetition pattern.

The same local tetrahedral relation can appear inside different global structures. Face Centered Cubic and Hexagonal Close Packing share the same close-packed local motif, while differing in layer stacking and global symmetry.

### Regular Kissing Tetrad Is Not Any Tetrahedral Cell

The Prismal Tetrad is a regular kissing simplex: four equal spheres, all mutually touching, with six equal contact edges among their centers.

Some lattice decompositions use tetrahedral cells in the weaker simplex sense of four non-coplanar points. Those cells may be irregular, may contain non-contact edges, or may connect points that are not nearest neighbors. Such cells should not be treated as Prismal Tetrads unless all six edges are equal nearest-neighbor sphere contacts.

Body Centered Cubic and simple cubic coordinate scaffolds can contain tetrahedral cells, and a cubic scaffold can contain regular tetrahedral point subconfigurations. That does not mean those tetrahedra are the nearest-neighbor contact primitive of the structure.

For example, a cube contains a regular tetrahedron if alternating corners are chosen. If Body Centered Cubic spheres are sized for nearest-neighbor contact between cube corners and body centers, those alternating cube-corner edges are not the contact edges. Enlarging those spheres until the alternating cube corners touch would overlap the body-center spheres.

### Lattice Is Not Coordinate System

A lattice may be represented with many coordinate systems.

For example, Face Centered Cubic can be represented with primitive lattice vectors, a conventional cubic cell, parity constraints inside an integer cubic scaffold, hexagonal layer coordinates, or barycentric simplex coordinates.

The coordinate representation is a chart over the structure. It is not the structure itself.

### Cubic Scaffold Is Not Ontology

A doubled or scaled integer cubic scaffold can expose simple parity classes for vertices, edge centers, face centers, body centers, and related cubic-incidence structures.

That scaffold is useful for implementation, debugging, and interoperation with Cartesian engines. It should not become the ontology of Prismal Space merely because it is convenient.

### Behavior Profile Is Not Substrate

Material-like behavior profiles can select preferred planes, directions, slip families, propagation rules, diffusion paths, or anisotropies over an existing structural representation.

Those profiles may be Face Centered Cubic-like, Hexagonal Close Packing-like, Body Centered Cubic-like, or something else. They should remain distinguishable from the substrate and from the views used to inspect it.

## Working Terms

### Prismal Tetrad

A local 3D relation among four mutually touching equal spheres. The four sphere centers form a regular tetrahedron.

Equivalently, the Prismal Tetrad is a regular kissing simplex: all four spheres touch all three of the others, and the six center-to-center contact edges are equal.

In this record, Prismal Tetrad names the local relational motif, not a global lattice.

### Simplex

The minimal non-degenerate shape in a given dimension.

- In 1D: a line segment.
- In 2D: a triangle.
- In 3D: a tetrahedron.

The Prismal Tetrad is the 3D regular kissing simplex motif relevant to local close packing.

Not every tetrahedral simplex in a lattice decomposition is a Prismal Tetrad.

### Simplex Complex

A structure made from simplices and their incidence relations.

For Prismal geometry research, a simplex complex can preserve which local sphere centers are mutually adjacent without requiring the whole space to be described first as a cubic or Cartesian grid.

### Close-Packed Layer

A 2D triangular arrangement of equal sphere centers in which each interior sphere touches six neighbors in the layer.

3D close-packed sphere arrangements can be built by stacking these layers so that each new layer sits in one of the tetrahedral void positions of the previous layer.

### Stacking Sequence

The sequence of layer offsets used to build a 3D close-packed arrangement.

Common symbolic layer names are A, B, and C, with the constraint that adjacent layers do not occupy the same offset position.

### Barlow Packing

A 3D close-packed sphere arrangement generated by stacking triangular layers using valid A/B/C layer choices.

Face Centered Cubic and Hexagonal Close Packing are the most familiar regular examples.

### Lattice Realization

A regular global realization of local constraints.

Face Centered Cubic and Hexagonal Close Packing are lattice realizations of close-packed sphere constraints. Other Barlow packings may be periodic, partially ordered, disordered, or mixed.

### View

A query, projection, coordinate chart, visualization, or derived representation over an underlying structure.

Views are useful and necessary, but they should preserve which assumptions they introduce.

## Close-Packed Family

### Face Centered Cubic

Face Centered Cubic is a regular close-packed lattice realization with ABCABC stacking of triangular layers.

It has 12 nearest neighbors per sphere and a rhombic dodecahedron Voronoi cell.

In current Prismal work, Face Centered Cubic remains the default initial 3D realization because it supports a rhombic dodecahedral cell view and relates cleanly to cubic engine coordinates.

### Hexagonal Close Packing

Hexagonal Close Packing is a regular close-packed lattice realization with ABAB stacking of triangular layers.

It also has 12 nearest neighbors per sphere and the same maximum sphere-packing density as Face Centered Cubic, but it has different global symmetry and different stacking context.

For Prismal research, Hexagonal Close Packing is important because it tests whether the substrate model has accidentally overfit Face Centered Cubic.

### Barlow Stackings

Barlow stackings generalize the Face Centered Cubic and Hexagonal Close Packing cases by allowing any valid sequence of A, B, and C close-packed layers where adjacent layers differ.

This makes the layer sequence itself first-class.

A representation that can handle Barlow stackings can distinguish local close-packing constraints from global regularity assumptions.

## Cubic and Body Centered Cubic Reference Views

### Simple Cubic

Simple cubic structure is useful as an indexing and engine-facing reference. It is not close packed and should not be treated as the Prismal substrate.

### Body Centered Cubic

Body Centered Cubic is useful as a comparison structure and may be available naturally inside cubic incidence representations that include both cube vertices and body centers.

However, Body Centered Cubic is not a Barlow close packing and does not share the same local 12-neighbor close-packed sphere arrangement as Face Centered Cubic or Hexagonal Close Packing.

Body Centered Cubic nearest-neighbor contacts occur between cube-corner and body-center sublattices. That nearest-neighbor contact graph is bipartite, so it does not contain the triangular contact loops or four-sphere complete contact graph required by a Prismal Tetrad.

Body Centered Cubic may contain tetrahedral decomposition cells or regular tetrahedral point subconfigurations when viewed through a cubic scaffold. Those are not Prismal Tetrads unless they are also regular nearest-neighbor kissing relations.

For Prismal, this means Body Centered Cubic is probably not the hard unification problem. The harder problem is preserving the distinction between local Prismal Tetrad relations, Face Centered Cubic global stacking, Hexagonal Close Packing global stacking, and broader Barlow sequences.

### Cubic Parity Scaffold

A doubled integer cubic scaffold can expose useful parity classes:

```text
even, even, even -> cubic vertices
odd, even, even  -> edge centers
even, odd, even  -> edge centers
even, even, odd  -> edge centers
odd, odd, even   -> face centers
odd, even, odd   -> face centers
even, odd, odd   -> face centers
odd, odd, odd    -> body centers
```

This makes cubic, Face Centered Cubic, and Body Centered Cubic relations easy to inspect in one implementation-friendly structure.

That convenience should be recorded as a view or scaffold, not as proof that cubic incidence is the foundation of Prismal geometry.

## Open Research Questions

### Minimal Distinguishing Cluster

What is the smallest observable local or near-local cluster that distinguishes Face Centered Cubic from Hexagonal Close Packing?

Because the first neighbor shell is the same size and very similar, the distinguishing evidence may require layer context beyond the immediate kissing neighbors.

### Tetrad Growth Rule

Can a Prismal Tetrad-first growth rule generate useful local test cases without committing to a full global lattice?

A useful test may begin with one regular kissing simplex, then add spheres into available tetrahedral voids while recording which choices lead toward ABCABC, ABAB, or other Barlow sequences.

Treating the three contact edges of one Prismal Tetrad as translation generators is a separate lattice commitment. That commitment produces Face Centered Cubic, but it should not be confused with the local Prismal Tetrad relation itself.

### Integer and Rational Test Vectors

Which integer or rational coordinate systems should be used for canonical test vectors?

Candidates include:

- Face Centered Cubic parity coordinates inside a cubic scaffold.
- Hexagonal layer coordinates plus integer stacking index.
- Barycentric coordinates inside tetrahedra.
- A shared scaled integer coordinate system for visualization and interop.

### Barlow Sequence Representation

Should Barlow sequences be represented as explicit layer strings, as local transition rules, as a graph of allowed layer offsets, or as a derived view over a simplex complex?

This remains open.

### Role of Body Centered Cubic

How should Body Centered Cubic be exposed in the test harness?

It may be best treated as an embedded cubic-incidence reference view rather than a peer close-packing realization.

### Engine Boundary

Where should the implementation boundary sit between PrismalCore and PrismalExperimental?

The likely split is:

- PrismalCore preserves exact local relations, coordinates, and invariants.
- PrismalExperimental renders scenes that expose lattice views, layer views, and comparison scaffolds.

This record does not decide that split.

## Test Harness Implications

The test harness should make assumptions visible rather than hiding them behind one default lattice view.

Candidate scenes or toggles:

- Show one Prismal Tetrad as the local 3D regular kissing simplex motif.
- Grow a small cluster by adding spheres into tetrahedral voids.
- Show triangular close-packed layers with A, B, and C offset positions.
- Compare ABCABC and ABAB stacking.
- Show Face Centered Cubic and Hexagonal Close Packing clusters with layer coloring.
- Show why first-neighbor shells alone may not distinguish stacking context.
- Show the cubic parity scaffold separately, with Face Centered Cubic and Body Centered Cubic toggles.
- Show regular tetrahedral point subconfigurations in a cubic scaffold separately from nearest-neighbor kissing tetrads.
- Show the rhombic dodecahedron Voronoi cell view for Face Centered Cubic.

The goal is not only to render attractive geometry. The goal is to make the consequences of each representation observable.
