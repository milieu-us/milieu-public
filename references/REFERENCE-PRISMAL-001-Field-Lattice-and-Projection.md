# REFERENCE-PRISMAL-001-Field-Lattice-and-Projection

## Field, Lattice, and Projection — Working Model

ID: REFERENCE-PRISMAL-001
Status: Working Draft
Created: 2026-04-03
Updated: 2026-04-04

## Intent
Capture an emerging framing for how continuous space, discrete simulation, and computational representations relate within Prismal work.

This is a working model. It is not a decision and remains subject to revision.

---

### Anchor

Prismal Space is a discrete realization of an underlying continuum.

---

### Underlying Continuum (Working Properties)

We assume an underlying continuum as the least-assumptive substrate from which discrete spatial structures may arise.

This continuum is characterized by:

- Directional neutrality (no privileged axes or orientations)
- Continuity (no inherent discretization)
- Local relationality (structure defined by relationships, not fixed adjacency)
- Composability (no fixed tiling or partitioning)
- Constraint responsiveness (structure may arise through constraints)
- Scale agnosticism (no inherent unit scale)

These properties are provisional and subject to revision.

---

### Discrete Realization (Prismal Space)

Prismal Space represents a discrete realization of the underlying continuum.

It is currently implemented using an FCC / rhombic dodecahedral lattice due to its symmetry and adjacency properties:

- Uniform local neighborhood (12 equidistant neighbors)
- Space-filling structure (via rhombic dodecahedral tiling)
- High degree of directional symmetry relative to axis-aligned grids

This discretization is not assumed to be unique or fundamental. It is treated as one valid realization among potentially many.

---

### Constraint and Discretization (Working Hypothesis)

We suspect that discrete spatial structures may arise as stable realizations of an underlying continuum under the influence of constraints.

This relationship is not yet formalized or proven. It is treated as a working hypothesis based on observed patterns across multiple domains.

---

### Implications (Open)

- Coordinate systems should be treated as projections, not foundational structure
- Discrete simulation may be grounded in locally isotropic structures rather than axis-aligned grids
- Multiple valid discretizations may exist depending on constraints
- Constraint Fabric may play a generative role in producing stable spatial structure

---

### Open Questions

**Continuum and Discretization**
- How should discrete realizations be derived from the continuum?
- What properties must any valid discretization preserve?
- Are multiple discretizations equally valid under different constraints?

**Constraint and Emergence**
- Under what conditions do stable discrete structures arise?
- Does discretization necessarily emerge from constraints, or is it one of several possible outcomes?
- Can multiple discrete realizations coexist or transition between one another?

**Scale and Representation**
- How should discretization behave across scale?
- When (if ever) should projection systems (e.g., Cartesian) be treated as authoritative?

---

### Influences (Non-exhaustive)

This work draws from established ideas in geometry, physics, and computational modeling, including:

- Continuous manifolds and local structure (e.g., Riemann)
- Symmetry-based reasoning (e.g., Noether, Klein)
- Sphere packing and lattice structures (e.g., Kepler, Gauss, Hales)

These influences inform, but do not define, the structures explored here.
