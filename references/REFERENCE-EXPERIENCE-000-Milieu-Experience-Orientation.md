## Intent

Preserve the core conceptual orientation of Milieu Experiences so they remain stable across implementation, tooling, and team evolution.

## Purpose

This document captures a **way of seeing** Milieu Experiences.

It is not a specification, architecture, or implementation guide.

It exists to:

- prevent conceptual drift
- align collaborators (human agents, AI agents, system agents)
- provide a stable reference for decision-making

> We preserve how to understand the system, not just how to build it.

---

## Why This Exists (Context for New Readers)

Most digital experiences today assume:

- a single, always-running timeline
- large, anonymous groups
- content consumed more than co-created

These assumptions make it hard to:

- form meaningful small-group experiences
- return after absence without "falling behind"
- treat player-created stories as legitimate

Milieu takes a different approach:

- small groups are the primary unit of experience
- time advances through participation, not in your absence
- multiple valid threads of reality can coexist
- past moments can be revisited, reinterpreted, and continued

This document exists to support:

- storytelling and lore that reflect these ideas
- systems that reinforce them rather than contradict them
- participants who can quickly understand "how this world works"

---

## Core Orientation

Milieu crafts experiences where agents (people, AI, and systems) participate together in shared space. In a Milieu experience, time, meaning, and even what is considered "real" emerges from that participation. These experiences can later be revisited, reinterpreted, and continued.

---

## Foundational Principles

### Agents are defined by participation, not implementation

Agents include:

- human agents (people)
- AI agents
- system agents (software, services)

An agent is anything that can take part in actions and contribute to shared meaning.

Not:

- just a user account
- just an AI model
- just a background process

But:

- an active participant in a shared experience

---

### Threads are lived experiences, not recordings

A thread is:

- a lived path through an experience
- a sequence of meaningful interactions between participants

Not:

- a replay file
- a raw log of actions

But:

- a structured memory of what was experienced, which can later be revisited and interpreted in new ways

---

### Time unfolds through participation

Time is not a single, always-running timeline.

- The world becomes active where people are paying attention
- Events happen because participants gather and engage

Not:

- “the world moves on without you”

But:

- time is experienced through participation

---

### Shared space anchors reality; time is contextual

- Places are consistent and recognizable
- Time can differ between groups and experiences

Not:

- one global, synchronized timeline

But:

- shared places with flexible, participant-relative time

---

### Multiple valid realities can coexist

- Different groups may experience different versions of events
- No single timeline defines an entire reality

Not:

- one authoritative story that everyone must follow

But:

- many valid experiences that share common elements

---

### Convergence creates shared memory

- Sometimes, multiple groups come together in the same place and moment
- These events create strong, shared memories across groups

Not:

- constant synchronization between all participants

But:

- meaningful moments of alignment

---

### Archive enables re-entry and reinterpretation

Archives are not just recordings of the past.

It is:

- a space that can be explored again (passively or actively)
- a source for reenactment and remix

Not:

- passive playback

But:

- an active way to return to and reinterpret past experiences

---

### Meaning is preserved over fidelity

Systems and formats will change over time.

We prioritize:

- preserving meaning
- keeping experiences understandable

Not:

- perfectly recreating every detail forever

But:

- making sure what was experienced can still be understood and rebuilt

---

### Threads belong to those who lived them

- Participants may carry, store, and re-host threads
- Persistence is shared, not centralized

Not:

- platform ownership of all data

But:

- stewardship of access

---

### Protocols enable composition across groups

- Protocols are shared patterns for how groups interact and coordinate
- They allow small groups to come together to share larger, meaningful events
- They support convergence without forcing everyone into the same structure

Not:

- rigid rules or top-down control

But:

- flexible agreements that enable trust and coordination

## Key Concepts (Plain Language)

These are simple definitions to help new readers understand the system:

### Thread

A path of experience created by people (and other agents) participating together.

### Event

Something meaningful that happens during an experience.

### Agent

Anything that can participate—such as a person, an AI, or a system.

### Place

A location in the shared world where experiences happen.

### Convergence

A moment when different groups come together and share the same experience.

### Archive

A preserved version of an experience that can be explored again later.

### Reenactment

Returning to a past moment and experiencing it again—possibly in a different way.

---

## Design Orientation (What We Optimize For)

Milieu does not optimize for:

- maximum concurrency
- continuous simulation
- singular canonical narratives

Milieu optimizes for:

- meaningful participation
- small group coherence -- while enabling protocols that allow groups to compose into larger, meaningful constellations at sentient scale
- repeatable engagement
- interpretability over time

---

## Misinterpretation Guards (Common Confusions)

### Threads are not logs

They are not raw data streams of actions.
They are structured representations of lived experience.

### Archive is not just replay

It is not limited to watching.
It enables exploration, reinterpretation, and creation.

### Agents are not AI

AI agents are one type among many.
Agents are defined by participation.

### Time is not continuous simulation

The system does not require a constantly running world.

### Multiple realities do not mean no structure

Shared anchors, convergence events, and lineage maintain coherence.
