## Proposal Summary

Participants perform live using their natural voice.

Dialogue is captured and selectively transformed into **character-consistent voices** while preserving:

- timing
- emotional delivery
- improvisational flow

This approach aims to combine:

- **live-play authenticity** (embodied improvisation)
- **production-level vocal consistency**

---

## Why This Matters (Experience Alignment)

Voice is a primary signal in embodied interaction. It carries:

- **status and intent**
- **character identity**
- **relational cues between participants**

Improvisational work suggests that embodiment deepens when participants can fully inhabit roles without self-conscious constraint.

Voice fonts may enable:

### Expanded Role Range

Participants can portray characters beyond their natural vocal range without strain.

### Preservation of Play

Improvisation remains live and responsive; transformation happens after capture.

### Continuity Across Sessions

Characters retain recognizable voices across sessions and formats.

### Lower Barrier to Entry

Participants do not need specialized voice acting skills to explore diverse roles.

---

## Role-State Signaling (Capture Requirement)

Participants must be able to explicitly indicate when they are speaking **in-character**.

## Initial Mechanism (Pilot)

- **Push-to-talk (PTT)** marks speech as *in-character*
- Speech outside PTT is treated as:
  - out-of-character (OOC), or
  - coordination/meta communication

## Why This Matters

- **Preserves intent** â€” participants control when they enter role
- **Prevents misclassification** â€” avoids transforming unintended speech
- **Supports safety boundaries** â€” clear separation between play and self
- **Improves processing accuracy** â€” removes need for unreliable inference

## Data Implication (Minimal)

Each audio segment is tagged at capture:

- speaker identity
- timestamp
- role state (in-character / out-of-character)

This becomes part of the **authoritative interaction record**.

---

## How It Works (Pilot Flow)

1. **Live Session**

   - Participants perform in real time
   - Audio is captured per participant
   - Role-state (PTT) is recorded alongside audio

2. **Transformation Pass**

   - Only **in-character segments** are processed
   - Dialogue is transformed into assigned character voices
   - Timing and expressive features are preserved

3. **Output**

   - Character-consistent audio for:
     - replay / archive
     - edited productions
     - future real-time exploration (optional)

## Constraints and Guardrails

### No Voice Cloning

- Do not replicate identifiable real voices
- Use only:
  - synthetic voices
  - licensed/generated profiles

### Participant Awareness

Participants must understand:

- their voice is being transformed
- how the output will be used

### Identity Integrity

Voice transformation must not:

- misrepresent participants
- remove authorship or agency

### Reversibility

Raw audio remains available as ground truth.

## Success Criteria (Initial)

The pilot is successful if:

- participants retain **full expressive control**
- transformed voices are **consistent and believable**
- role-state signaling is **usable without breaking flow**
- cost remains **predictable and manageable**
- no safety or identity concerns arise
